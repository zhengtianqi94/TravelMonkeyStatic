Everything You Want To Know About Penang – Places, Accommodation & Food

10 - 04 -2017
Author

Penang is popularly dubbed as ‘The Pearl of the Orient’ by foreign tourists and travelers, indeed an amazing place to explore. Featuring old-fashioned allure plus an interesting fusion of local and eclectic cultures, the island attracts hundreds and thousands of visitors every year. With a plethora of historical attractions, its capital has earned UNESCO World Heritage status because of its 19th and early 20th-century architecture. Contact Traveloka Malaysia right away to explore the island and a province stretch along the northwestern coast of Malaysia. The 10-mile journey over aquamarine waters offers plenty of time for the tourists to admire the mountains of the uninhabited jungle and the approaching cityscape. Penang is indeed like Mary Poppins, with loads of colors and unexpected surprises that fit in small dimensions.

Start with exploring the art and architecture of Penang that is brimming with rustic charm. You are sure to come across crumbling pre-war colonial buildings amidst the unique cultural and architectural landscape. It is easy to notice the influence of British colonialism. The major sights are not just confined to architectural buildings but are also present along the sides and walls of buildings as paintings, murals, and wall art. One of the most popular beach destination for tourists is Batu Ferringhi, because of its long stretch of sandy shores. Tanjung Bungah, located closely to Georgetown, is known for its lush greenery and rocky landscapes. Another of the most well-known places in Penang is Gurney. Bayan Lepas, home to the Free Industrial Zone has its own charms.

Dining in Penang is indeed a great gastronomic experience. It is famous for its hawker street food that shows a fusion of Chinese, Indian and Malay cuisines. Most of the travelers are seen to enjoy Penang’s food scene with its mouth-watering concoction of flattened rice noodles with spring onions, prawns, cockles, bean sprouts, and egg. Nasi Kandar is another extremely popular Penang dish which consists of white rice served with curries and vegetables. Do not forget to taste Cendol, a dessert made of ice flavored with coconut milk, Melaka sugar, and pandan jelly. Go ahead and relish the regional food paradise, with a rich cuisine. Penangites are known for their obsession with discovering the best flavors from different cuisines. Some of the must-eats in Penang include Assam Laksa, Penang Rojak, Assam Laksa, Penang Teochew Cendol and Char KwayTeow.
